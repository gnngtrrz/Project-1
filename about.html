About

Founding (1964): Nike was originally founded as "Blue Ribbon Sports" by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight at the University of Oregon. They initially operated as a distributor for the Japanese shoemaker Onitsuka Tiger (now ASICS). Name Change (1971): The company officially became Nike, Inc. in 1971, named after the Greek winged goddess of victory. Swoosh Logo (1971): The iconic Nike Swoosh logo was created by graphic designer Carolyn Davidson, and it became a symbol of the brand's identity.

Waffle Sole (1972): Bill Bowerman developed the innovative waffle sole, featuring a pattern inspired by his wife's waffle iron, which provided better traction for running shoes. This design innovation helped establish Nike's reputation for cutting-edge technology. Air Cushioning (1978): Nike introduced the Air cushioning technology, which revolutionized the sneaker industry by providing enhanced comfort and performance. "Just Do It" Slogan (1988): Nike launched its famous "Just Do It" marketing campaign, which became one of the most recognizable and enduring advertising slogans in history.

Michael Jordan Partnership (1984): Nike signed a groundbreaking endorsement deal with basketball superstar Michael Jordan, leading to the creation of the Air Jordan line of sneakers. This partnership was a game-changer for sports marketing. Expansion into Other Sports (1980s-1990s): Nike expanded its product offerings beyond running and basketball, entering various sports markets, including soccer, tennis, golf, and more. Acquisitions and Subsidiaries (Various): Nike acquired several companies, including Converse (2003) and Hurley International (2002), to diversify its brand portfolio.

Global Expansion (1980s-Present): Nike expanded its global reach, becoming a dominant force in the international athletic footwear and apparel markets. Athlete Endorsements (Ongoing): Nike has continued to partner with world-class athletes and teams across various sports, further strengthening its global presence and market share. Cultural Influence (Ongoing): Nike remains a dominant force in popular culture, collaborating with artists, musicians, and designers, and supporting social and cultural movements.
